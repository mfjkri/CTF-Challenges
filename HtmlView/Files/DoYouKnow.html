QWERTY vs Dvorak


QWERTY keyboard was invented together with the first commercial typewriter. To overcome the issue where the bars used to collide with each other, the keys were arranged in a pattern where the most commonly used letters were spread apart.
The Dvorak keyboard layout tries to minimize the distance traveled by the fingers. It also tries to make the typist alternate hands on consecutive letters as often as possible.
Many will argue it is more efficient than QWERTY.
